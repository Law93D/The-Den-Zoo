BLACK MAMBAS

The black mamba is one of the fastest and most feared snakes in the world. Despite its name, it’s not black—its skin is olive-gray, but the inside of its mouth is deep black. Black mambas are incredibly agile and can slither at speeds up to 20 km/h. They live in savannas and rocky areas across sub-Saharan Africa and feed on small mammals and birds.

Though dangerous due to their potent venom, black mambas are shy and avoid humans when possible. They play a vital role in the ecosystem by controlling rodent populations. These snakes are not aggressive unless threatened, and most bites occur when they are surprised or cornered.
